    Overview

    I'm currently working as a research fellow at CNRS@CREATE (Singapore) in DesCartes program, I obtained my Ph.D. degree in science and technology of information and communication from Laboratory of Signals and Systems (L2S, UMR 8506) , Université Paris Saclay, France, 2023. I received the bachelor’s and master’s degrees in control science and engineering from Guangdong University of Technology, Guangzhou, China, in 2016 and 2019, respectively.

    My current research interests include Anomaly Detection, Fault Diagnosis, One-Class Classification, Physics-Informed Learning, Statistic Learning, Deep learning, Signal Processing

    Research Topics

  • Faults Detection and localization on power grid using one-class classification methods with physics-informed learning

    This topic is focused on detecting and localizing high impedance fault occured in power distribution grid by using hybrid AI approaches.
    We developed a physics-informed one-class classification model for detection task by employing an Auto-encoder with an ellipse constraint.
    Our next step involves integrating grid topology information into our learning models to accurately localize nodes related to faults.

  • One-class classificarion approaches for incipient fault diagnosis in multivariate systems

    This topic explores comprehensive approaches for incipient fault diagnosis in multivariate systems, encompassing three key tasks: fault detection, identification of the faulty variables, and estimation of fault amplitudes.
    We have introduced a highly sensitive solution for the fault detection, utilizing an empirical probability density estimation technique with the cumulative sum method.
    Secondly, we developed a new one-class classification approach that determines a precise healthy region with multiple centers and a region radius.
    Based on this decision boundary, we defined the Local Mahalanobis Distance(LMD) and proposed the robust anchor-generation algorithm to implement the healthy region modeling.
    Diagnostic solutions for the three aforementioned fault diagnosis tasks have been formulated based on the LMD index.

  • Open circuit fault diagnosis for interleaved DC-DC converters

    We developed the system mode for multi-phase interleaved DC-DC converters and used the state variable as a fault detection indicator.
    Additionally, we created both a simulation environment and an experimental setup to validate the method's effectiveness.

  • Receiver Operation Characteristic (ROC) analysis and its applications

    We introduced a rapid algorithm for estimating the area under the ROC curve, applied ROC analysis to address change point detection, and subsequently crafted Python packages and software for multi-class ROC analysis
